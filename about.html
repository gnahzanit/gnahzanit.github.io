ABOUT ME

I recently graduated from University of Toronto with an Honours Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Science, English, and Mathematics.

I love art and have always wanted to make my own games. At fifteen, I sought to break into the game industry through concept art.

However, after thoughtful consideration and advice from my incredible mentors, I was made aware that this path can be risky and unstable, especially for international students.

Therefore, I made the sudden but pragmatic choice to pursue computer science in my last year of high school, aiming to keep game development and art as hobbies until I secured a stable job.

With little differing perspectives at university, which mainly favoured software engineering and research, I promptly chose software engineering to pursue as my career, overlooking how it did not suit my strengths.

So I struggled for all four years of university.

After school ended around May of 2024, I took a four months break in my hometown of Shanghai, China to obtain permanent residency in the United States.

This break allowed me to reevaluate my passions and goals. I realized that making video games is about one of the few things I really care about (besides cats, of course).

And now, I am ready & excited to be a part of the game industry :)

WHAT DO GAMES MEAN TO ME

I see games as an art form.

Games are an art form that is more adaptive, dimensional, expressive, and expansive than ever before.

My purpose as an artist is to craft games that excel technically while preserving the profound, unique, but analogous human touch.
